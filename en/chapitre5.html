In Crete, Minos’s fury fell on Daedalus, who had betrayed both his trust and the laws of hospitality. He seized the inventor and his son Icarus, and threw them into the labyrinth. Alas, the great architect could never have imagined that he would be a victim of his own invention: for even without the Minotaur, his labyrinth was a prison from which nobody could escape.

Like Birds
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
However, Daedalus had more than one trick up his sleeve. He gathered the feathers lost by birds flying over the labyrinth, and a bee’s nest gave him the other necessary ingredient: wax as the perfect glue. Daedalus made wings for both himself and Icarus. He glued them to their arms. Waving them like birds, the two prisoners flew away in the air!
In your opinion, is attaching feathers to your arms enough to enable you to fly? If you want to try, you run the risk of being disappointed. You will find, in “The Clever Corner”, the history of flying machines.
Icarus’s fall
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
For the first time since Bellerophon had tamed Pegasus, two human beings could fly through the air by their own means. Carried away by the incredible sensations he was feeling, Icarus wanted to go even higher, and even reach the sun! He did not hear the warnings of his father who begged him to be reasonable, and so he beat and beat his wings! Alas, when he came close to the fire cart of the sun, the wax that glued his wings started to melt. Powerless, Daedalus saw his son plummet into the sea and perish.
For the ancient Greeks the sun was a fire ball which moved from the east to the west across the sky. They did not know that the earth was a sphere which revolves around the sun.
King Minos’s Death
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
Minos did not intend to let Daedalus get off so easily. He put spies on the old scientist’s heels until he knew that he had taken shelter in Sicily, with king Cocalus. Minos gathered his fleet and crossed the Mediterranean. If Cocalus did not hand him Daedalus, then it would be war! The king of Sicily was certainly not strong enough to face Minos’s armies, so he pretended to agree and invited him to his palace. The princesses prepared a bath for him and the king of Crete got into it to rest from his long crossing. Thus he was the victim of Daedalus’s ruse: the bath was a trap and when Minos had settled inside it, it filled with boiling water.
Thus perished Minos, son of Zeus and king of Crete, in his ninety-ninth year of age. His reign had been the longest and the most glorious ever known since the creation of the world. He had always shown strictness but also fairness, except on a unique occasion which was the cause of his misfortunes and downfall.
Sicily is the biggest island in the Mediterranean. In ancient times many Greeks lived there. Today Sicily is part of Italy.

King Theseus
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
The Athenians put Theseus on the throne. The Queen and Prince Medus accepted his authority. He did not want to be a kind but feeble sovereign like Aegeus, nor severe though strong like Minos. In fact, he did not want the destiny of a whole city to depend on the character and moods of only one man, even if that man were the king, or himself. Therefore he established the first code of laws to rule the governing of the city, under the control and direction of an assembly elected by the people. Athenian democracy was born, and it was the very first in the world. And it was the king who served the people and no longer the people in the service of the king.

The Adventure Continued
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
However, Theseus could not remain still. He was missing something which he despaired of finding. He set out on the roads and started having adventures again. He fought other enemies and faced other perils. He met other women and got married … several times. But never again did he experience the love and internal peace he had known with Adriane when they were together on the boat fleeing from Crete.
Theseus’s adventures will be numerous and they will take him very far away, up to the kingdom of the dead. You will meet him again in our book “The Twelve Labors of Hercules”.
Adriane and Dionysus
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
After Theseus had fled from her, Adriane grieved for months on the isle of Naxos and shed floods of tears while pulling out her hair. Meanwhile Dionysus’s procession passed by, cleaving the waves, and the god was charmed by the beauty and the misfortunes of the Cretan princess. He invited her to his suite and married her. Later on he asked Zeus, his father, to grant him the favor of making her immortal. He accepted and transformed her into a star. If you look for her, she still shines some nights.
Dionysus is Zeus’s son, the god of wine and dance. You will read his story in our book “The Olympians”.
Minos, Judge of the Abysses
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
Minos was dead but the gods had not finished with him. They appointed him as a judge in the Abysses, together with Aeacus and Rhadamanthus. He weighs the good and bad actions of people after they die, and according to whether the scales tilt towards one side or the other, he sends them to either the Isle of the Blessed or to Tartarus. Sitting on his throne, at the entrance of Hades’s kingdom, he waits: sooner or later, Daedalus and Theseus, those old acquaintances of his, will stand before him and submit to his judgment. He will then remain fair and strict, the way he has always been.
The Abysses are the underworld of the dead where the god Hades, Zeus and Poseidon’s brother, is reigning. You will learn more about this god by reading “The Creation of the World”.

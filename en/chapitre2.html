Aegeus and Aethra
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
Aegeus was the king of Athens. Contrary to Minos he was neither strict nor fair, but was gentle and weak, maybe too much so for a king. While visiting the king of the little town called Trezene, he fell under the charm of princess Aethra, whom he also pleased. However, their marriage was impossible, as Minos would never accept the union of two Greek princes, as they might form a greater kingdom than his own. Therefore, Aegeus had to go back to Athens, leaving the pregnant Aethra behind. But before leaving, he placed a golden sword and sandals under a heavy stone. He ordered the princess to tell his child the truth once he was old and strong enough to lift the stone.
The Delphic Oracle was dedicated to Apollo. There, Pythia answered all the questions people asked. Aegeus came to consult her, as he wanted to know whether he could have an heir or not.

Theseus’ s childhood
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
Aethra gave birth to a son she called Theseus. She brought him up with care and love. the very beginning he was a prodigious boy, wise like a priest, skillful and strong like a hunter, and handsome as a god! He excelled in everything, yet remained simple and upright. When he became an adult, Aethra showed him the stone to lift and explained his origins to him. Theseus decided to leave for Athens. In Trezene everybody mourned him: such a strong and handsome prince would have been the glory of his people!
The education of young Greeks was very complete: they studied science, arts and also sports, which were considered as important as intellectual disciplines.

On the way
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
On the way to Athens there were bandits who terrorized the region. Aegeus was too feeble and undecided to defeat them. Theseus could not bear this injustice: he hunted them and killed them all! The cruelest of them was Procustes who had invented a terribly cruel trap. He posed as an inn-keeper. When his guests lay down on a bed, they got trapped. If they were bigger than the mattress he cut off their feet, and if they were smaller, he quartered them so as to fill the bed! But from head to feet, Theseus was exactly the right size. He liberated himself from the bed and with a stroke of his sword freed Greece from Procustes.
The prince meets his father
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
When he finally reached Athens, Theseus had lost some time on the way but he had covered himself with glory, and the people was talking about nobody but him. “Did you know that one single man freed the Attic from the murderers who raged the roads?” “Yes, and he is as handsome as the god Apollo himself!” “But where does he come from? And what is he here for?”
Theseus introduced himself to the court of king. Aegeus recognized him thanks to his golden sandals and sword. They fell into each other’s arms. Everything was absolutely perfect, wasn’t it? Not really, as Aegeus had another son, Medus. Theseus was the elder, but Medus was the legitimate prince. How to decide between them?
At that time in Greece, kings’ sons inherited their father’s thrones. That system was not satisfactory: what if the son did not have the required qualities to lead a country?

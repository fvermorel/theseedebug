The tribute of Athens
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
Athens had been defeated years before in a war against king Minos. Every year, the city had to deliver seven young men and seven young girls as a tribute. These unfortunate young people were handed over to the Minotaur as food! The queen, who wanted to get rid of Theseus in order to guarantee the throne of her son Medus, suggested the following solution: since Theseus was such a valorous hero, couldn’t he go to Crete and free Athens from this curse? The young prince, to the great despair of his father Aegeus, followed his courage and accepted the mission without any hesitation.
The son of Minos, Androgeus, had been assassinated in Athens, and Aegeus could not find the murderers. It was for this reason that Crete waged war against Athens and defeated it.
Heading for Crete
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
Among Athens’ people, enthusiasm was mixed with fear: should they run the risk of losing an exceptional prince just a few days after getting him as a present from the gods? Many young people offered to take Theseus’s place, and many fathers put forward their own sons. It reinforced Theseus’s determination even more: for the good of the Athenian people he would be ready to face any danger! But Aegeus remained inconsolable. He made Theseus promise that if he was victorious, he would hoist white sails when returning. That way he would see the boat from afar and his anxious wait would be shortened. If the sails remained black he would then know that his son had failed and been killed.

At King Minos’s Court
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
Thanks to favorable winds, the crossing was short. Theseus and his companions entered Minos’s palace. The beauty and the courage of the prince impressed the king of Crete and caused him bitter jealousy at the same time. Shabby Aegeus’s infidelity had given birth to a magnificent young man, and his wife’s to a monster! Never mind! Tomorrow the monster would devour the prince! For his last night; Minos ordered that Theseus should be treated with all the honors his rank deserved.
Among the Greeks, hospitality was sacred. Even though he was his enemy, Minos had to respect Theseus and care for him as long as he was in his house.

Adriane fell in Love
⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁⠁
But it was not only the king of Crete who was impressed by Theseus; his daughter Adriane, too, fell under the charm of the Athenian prince. Nobody had ever dared look her father in the eyes. Yet the young man, without trembling at the announcement of his imminent death, stood up to him without batting an eyelid. She fell immediately and deeply in love with him, and while she was showing him to his room, she declared her fervor to him. Theseus was touched by the passion and the beauty of the young girl. He promised her that he would marry her and they would flee together if he could get out of the labyrinth alive. However, though he had confidence in his lucky star, he did not have any idea how he would manage.
